The Gap Nobody Talks About

The support shifts.
The challenges don't.

During treatment, a whole team surrounds you. Then it ends — and your oncologist's focus rightly shifts to patients still in active care. The visits get further apart.

That transition happens at exactly the moment when things still aren't right. Fatigue persists. Brain fog is real. Sleep is disrupted. Strength hasn't returned. Side effects from treatment are still showing up — sometimes ones that weren't there before. And yet everyone around you expects you to be who you were before.

Managing these effects, rebuilding physical and mental health, understanding what's normal and what needs attention — this is real, practical work. It requires guidance, structure, and someone in your corner who understands survivorship specifically. That's what Life After Survivor provides.

How It Works

A structured path from
treatment to recovery.

Survivorship is its own chapter — with its own side effects, physical limitations, and mental health challenges. Managing fatigue, rebuilding strength, addressing brain fog — this is structured, practical work. Our process gives it the structure it deserves.

01

Free Discovery Call

A conversation about your current side effects, physical and mental health challenges, and what you most want to rebuild. No commitment required.

02

Personal Evaluation & Onboarding

Your care coordinator conducts a thorough conversation about your recovery status, goals, and daily challenges — then sets up your dedicated Slack channel so your team is ready.

03

Your Recovery Roadmap

A structured, practical plan built around managing what's lingering and rebuilding what treatment affected — with specialists matched to your specific needs.

04

Ongoing Check-ins & Adjustments

Regular check-ins to track progress and adjust your plan. Between sessions, use Slack to flag a question — your care coordinator responds during business hours.

Dedicated Care Coordinator

A real person who knows your story — phone, video, and Slack during business hours. Not immediate, but genuinely responsive.

Regular, Responsive Support

Your care coordinator responds to messages and calls during business hours. Have a question between sessions? Send it via Slack — no waiting until the next scheduled call.

Slack Team Channel

Every plan includes a dedicated Slack channel with your care team. Send a message whenever something comes up — your coordinator will respond during business hours.

Areas of Care

Managing side effects.

01

Follow-Up Care Navigation

Know what questions to ask at follow-up appointments, what side effects to flag, and how to turn your doctor's guidance into a practical daily plan.

02

Mental Health & Cognitive Recovery

Practical support for brain fog, anxiety, sleep disruption, and the mental fatigue that often follows treatment — with strategies you can use every day.

03

Nutrition Guidance

Personalized nutrition support for post-treatment recovery — managing weight changes, rebuilding energy, addressing appetite issues, and fueling daily life.

04

Exercise Physiology & Movement

Clinical exercise guidance to safely rebuild strength, stamina, and physical capacity after treatment — at a pace matched to your current recovery.

05

Physical Therapy Guidance

Telehealth PT consultation addressing pain, mobility limitations, and physical side effects of treatment that are getting in the way of daily activities.

06

Ergonomics — Work & Daily Life

Ergonomics isn't just for the office. Guidance covers workstation setup, job tasks, household chores, and everyday activities — small adjustments that reduce fatigue and discomfort.

07

Social Work & Family Support

Licensed social workers facilitate individual survivorship support and structured family sessions — covering communication, role adjustment, and caregiver support.

08

Life Reintegration Planning

A structured, practical roadmap for resuming work, family, hobbies, and daily activities — built around your current capacity and adjusted as you rebuild.

09

Return-to-Work Support

Guidance on communicating with employers, accommodation strategies, pacing your return, and managing the practical realities of rejoining the workforce after treatment.

About the Founders
Rick & Holly Tyler
Founders, Life After Survivor

Rick Tyler founded Life After Survivor for a deeply personal reason: his wife Holly is a cancer survivor. He lived through the fear, the uncertainty, and the painful gap in support that families face once treatment ends. Holly Tyler is co-founder — bringing her own lived experience of survivorship to every aspect of the program.

With over 20 years in ergonomics and occupational health and more than a decade focused on cancer survivorship, Rick built this program because he couldn't find one that actually addressed what survivors face in the months and years after treatment.
